# AI Brew Known Limits

AI Brew is a strong starting recipe and dial-in coach. It is not a claim that every coffee will taste ideal without real brewing.

Known limits:

- Beans vary by roast development, density, age, storage, and farm lot behavior.
- Process and variety labels are often incomplete or marketing-oriented.
- Grinder settings need calibration for burr zero point, burr wear, alignment, RPM, and retention.
- Water labels and public data can be incomplete or outdated.
- Espresso needs real basket, grinder, pressure, puck prep, and yield feedback.
- Batch brewer results depend on model, spray pattern, bed depth, filter basket, and thermal behavior.
- Siphon and moka depend heavily on heat control and operator timing.
- Cold brew depends on steep temperature, food-safety handling, filtration, and dilution preference.
- Mobile/PWA tests catch layout and flow regressions, but real device QA remains required for native store release.

Safe claim: AI Brew gives a guarded baseline and one-variable correction loop. Real taste feedback is still required.
